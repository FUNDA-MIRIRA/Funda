TERMINATION OF PREGNANCY



    Effective starting 1 January 1978, it was retained after Zimbabwe's independence in 1980. The law expanded abortion access, permitting it under three circumstances. “No person may terminate a pregnancy otherwise than by this Act. Any person who contravenes subsection shall be guilty of an offense and liable to a fine not exceeding level ten or to imprisonment for a period not exceeding five years or to both such fine and such imprisonment.” Termination of Pregnancy Act No. 15 10 of the Republic of Zimbabwe of 1977 with amendments through 2001.

    Zimbabwe ranks as one of the countries with the highest maternal mortality ratios in the world, estimated 651 maternal deaths per 100,000 live births. In contrast to a worldwide trend of decline, maternal mortality has increased in Zimbabwe over the past 25 years.

    Abortion is legally permitted in the country only under limited circumstances, including if the pregnant woman’s life is in danger or in cases of rape, incest, or fetal impairment. In practice, it is extremely difficult to obtain a legal abortion; as a result, most abortions are potentially unsafe.

    The Ministry of Health and Child Care of Zimbabwe has increased access to and improved post-abortion care to reduce maternal mortality. However, in such an effort to reduce the MMR the law still distracts the success, thus, empowerment of Adolescent Girls and Young Women with this law fuels informed decisions and capacitates them with advocacy skills for the amendment of this law.
