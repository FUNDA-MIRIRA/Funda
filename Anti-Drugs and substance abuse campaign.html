ANTI - DRUGS AND SUBSTANCE ABUSE CAMPAIGN

A certain study indicated that the effects of drug abuse include addiction, violence, high-risk sexual behavior, hospitalization for overdoses with some succumbing to drug-relate-dated diseases, poor academic performance, school dropout, and dependency on drugs, among others.

In most secondary schools the power of guidance and counseling was noted through the teachers' involvement in community programs targeted at reducing drug, and substance abuse both within the school premises and in the community at large. The pandemic has contributed to a mental health crisis in Zimbabwe driven by substance abuse, say health professionals.

Authorities and campaigners attribute it to loss of employment, prolonged school closures, and anxiety resulting from COVID-19. Research shows that at Ngomahuru Psychiatric Hospital in Masvingo, one of the country's biggest, 119 patients in acute ward, only 117 are a result of drug abuse and the majority of the patients are students. The Zimbabwe Civil Liberties and Drug Network 2017 mentioned 60% of youth (15-24 years) are using drugs and are affected by them.
